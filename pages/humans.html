Humans


Description

The Humans of Ancient Greece are a diverse and resilient race, known for their adaptability and versatility. They are proud and ambitious people, driven by a desire for knowledge, adventure, and power.

Humans come in a variety of shapes and sizes, with a wide range of skin colors, hair colors, and eye colors. They are known for their quick wit and intelligence and are often seen as the most versatile of all the races in Ancient Greece.

Humans are often quick to adapt to new situations, and they are not afraid to take risks. They are natural leaders, and they have a strong sense of community and family. They are also known for their love of knowledge and their desire to learn and explore.

In combat, humans are versatile and resourceful. They can use a wide range of weapons and armor, and they are quick to adapt to new situations. They are not as strong as some of the other races in Ancient Greece, but they make up for this with their cunning and strategic thinking.

Humans are a proud and ambitious race, and they are often seen as the driving force behind the major events and conflicts in Ancient Greece. They are often at the forefront of battles and adventures, and they are not afraid to take on the gods themselves if they believe it is necessary.

Despite their ambition, humans are also known for their compassion and their love of justice. They are often seen as the protectors of the weak and the defenders of the innocent, and they are not afraid to stand up for what is right, even in the face of great danger.

Traits:

  • Leadership: Humans are natural leaders, and they have a strong sense of community and family. They have a +2 bonus to Charisma and can inspire others to follow them.
  • Compassion: Humans are known for their compassion and their love of justice. They have a +2 bonus to Wisdom and are often seen as the protectors of the weak and the defenders of the innocent.
    • Vigilant Guardian: Once a creature that you can see within 15ft of you is hit with an attack roll, you can use your reaction to swap places with the creature, and you are hit by the attack instead. Once you use this trait, you can't do so again until you finish a long rest.
  • Love of Knowledge: Humans are driven by a desire for knowledge, and they are always seeking to learn and explore. They have a +2 bonus to Intelligence and can quickly understand new information.
    • Sentinel's Intuition: Whenever you make a Wisdom (Insight) or a Wisdom (Perception) check, you can roll a d4 and add the number rolled to the total ability check.
  • Base Speed: 30ft
  • Size: 5-7ft; Medium
  • Languages: Common, 1 Unique
