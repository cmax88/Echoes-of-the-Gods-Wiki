Worship

The Basics

Through worship, a mortal can gain divine abilities in which will assist them throughout their journeys across Gaea. These traits need to be earned though, whether it be through a good deed or an unjust action. Worship is measured through Piety Points, the amount of which is decided by the DM for each divine action completed by a character. Every Piety Point goes toward the player’s Piety Level, each major milestone being 3, 10, 25, and 50 Piety Points.

Zeus, God of the Sky:

    Earning Piety:

    • Solving a riddle or puzzle
    • Smiting the foolish and unwise in Zeus’s name
    • Helping a polis successfully plan or adapt to a threat
    • Building or restoring temple to Zeus

    Losing Piety:

    • Jeopardizing others through foolish actions
    • Willingly subverting or impeding a wise course of action
    • Failing to plan appropriately for a challenge
    • Giving into fury and destruction

Piety Awards:

  • Zeus’s Favor (3+ Piety)
    • Once on each of your turns when you hit a creature with an attack, you can deal an extra 1d6 lightning damage to the target. You can use this ability a number of times equal to your Intelligence modifier. Replenishes on a successful long rest.
  • Zeus’s Thunderchild (10+ Piety)
    • When you fail an Intelligence or a Wisdom saving throw, you can reroll the die, and you must use the new roll. Replenishes on a successful long rest.
  • Zeus’s Avatar (25+ Piety)
    • The energy of the skies fills your being, granting you immediate victory on initiative rolls.
    • Thunderstruck (Action):
      • Effect: Once per day, call down a bolt of lightning to smite your enemies.
      • Mechanics:
        • Roll a d20 to hit your target with a ranged spell attack.
        • Damage: 4d8 lightning damage on a successful hit.
  • Champion of the Skies (50+ Piety)
    • You can increase your Intelligence or Wisdom score by 4 and also increase your maximum for that score by 4.
    • Wrath of the Sky (Action):
      • Effect: Once per long rest, assume an avatar form, increasing your size and strength.
      • Mechanics:
        • You grow by one size category (e.g., from Medium to Large).
        • Your melee attacks deal an additional 4d10 lightning damage.
        • You gain resistance to non-magical damage.
        • Last until damage is taken

Poseidon, God of the Sea:

    Earning Piety:

    • Supporting those who would reform or overturn institutions
    • Preventing cataclysmic change
    • Offering a treasure to the sea
    • Defending or maintaining a temple of Poseidon

    Losing Piety:

    • Trying to keep a secret from party members
    • Calming the sea’s fury
    • Upholding an institution not devoted to Poseidon
    • Bowing to the desires or demands of another God

Piety Awards:

  • Poseidon’s Blessing (3+ Piety)
    • You can cast fog cloud, giving you the ability to encase anything within a 20ft radius of you in fog. Last 1 full round. Fog cast in this way smells strongly of the sea. You can cast this spell a number of times equal to your Intelligence modifier. Replenishes on a successful long rest.
  • Poseidon’s Disciple (10+ Piety)
    • You can cast blink, giving you the ability to teleport up to 30ft away from your current position. Does not provoke opportunity attacks. Replenishes on a successful long rest.
  • Poseidon’s Avatar (25+ Piety)
    • You are inspired by the tempestuous, uncontrollable nature of the sea; you have advantage on saving throws against being charmed or restrained.
    • Call of the Depths (Action):
      • Effect: Once per long rest, you can unleash the fury of the sea, creating a powerful whirlpool.
      • Mechanics:
        • Roll a d20 to target a 20-foot radius area.
        • Damage: 1d8 bludgeoning damage and 2d6 cold damage.
        • Targets must make a Strength saving throw (DC 17) or be pulled into the whirlpool, taking damage each turn while restrained.
  • Champion of the Seas (50+ Piety)
    • You can increase your Dexterity or Intelligence score by 4 and also increase your maximum for that score by 4.
    • Wrath of the Tides (Action):
      • Effect: Once per long rest, assume an avatar form, becoming a manifestation of Poseidon's power.
      • Mechanics:
        • You grow by one size category (e.g., from Medium to Large).
        • Your melee attacks deal an additional 2d12 cold and 2d10 bludgeoning damage.
        • You gain immunity to cold damage.
        • Last until damage is taken

Hades, God of the Underworld:

    Earning Piety:

    • Providing coins and overseeing burial rites for those slain
    • Ensuring that the deeds and knowledge of someone who died are preserved
    • Slaying anyone who has done evil towards the innocent

    Losing Piety:

    • Denying a dying person their final rites
    • Removing wealth from a corpse or defiling a tomb
    • Aiding those who do not accept the Underworld

Piety Awards:

  • Servant of the Underworld (3+ Piety)
    • You can cast gentle repose, giving you the ability to prevent a corpse from becoming undead. You can cast this spell a number of times equal to your Wisdom modifier. Replenishes on a successful long rest.
  • Emissary of the Afterlife (10+ Piety)
    • You can cast speak with dead, giving you the ability to gain the intelligence of a corpse within 10ft, allowing it to answer any questions you pose. Replenishes on a successful long rest.
  • Guardian of Souls (25+ Piety)
    • You can cast false life, giving you the ability to gain 25 temporary hit points. Replenishes on a successful long rest.
    • Soul Harvest (Action):
      • Effect: Once per long rest, you can use your connection to the underworld to drain the life force from a target.
      • Mechanics:
        • Make a melee attack roll against a target.
        • On a hit, deal 2d8 necrotic damage, and you regain hit points equal to half the damage dealt.
  • Champion of the Underworld (50+ Piety)
    • You can increase your Intelligence or Wisdom score by 4 and also increase your maximum for that score by 4.
    • Summon Undead (Action):
      • Effect: Once per long rest, you can summon a minor undead creature to serve you.
      • Mechanics:
        • Choose a type of minor undead (e.g., skeleton, zombie, or shadow).
        • The summoned undead obeys your commands and remains for 1 hour.
        • Undead must be a known creature to the player

Ares, God of War:

    Earning Piety:

    • Defeating a champion
    • Taking vengeance against a powerful foe
    • Burning a polis or a settlement to the ground
    • Desecrating a temple or tomb

    Losing Piety:

    • Failing to carry out a sworn vendetta
    • Publicly displaying weakness or compassion
    • Rejecting a duel or a challenge out of fear

Piety Awards:

  • Ares’s Aspirant (3+ Piety)
    • You can call on Ares favor and cast wrathful smite; the next time you are hit with a melee attack, you deal an extra 1d6 of psychic damage. This blessing also manifests a blood-red glow around your weapon, causing it to cast a dim light in a 10ft radius until you re-enter a bright location. Can be used a number of times equal to your Constitution modifier. Replenishes on a successful long rest.
  • Soldier of Ares (10+ Piety)
    • You can cast blinding smite, dealing an additional 3d8 radiant damage on a successful hit. Replenishes on a successful long rest.
  • Ares’s Avatar (25+ Piety)
    • Countless battles and vicious visions have given you focus. You have advantage on saving throws against being charmed or frightened.
    • War God’s Blessing (Bonus Action):
      • Effect: Three times a day, you can call upon Ares's blessing to ensure victory in combat.
      • Mechanics:
        • Roll a d20 before making an attack roll, saving throw, or ability check.
        • You can add a bonus equal to the roll to the result of your roll.
  • Champion of Slaughter (50+ Piety)
    • You can increase your Strength or Constitution score by 4 and also increase your maximum for that score by 4.
    • Ares’s Fury (Action):
      • Effect: Once per long rest, you can assume an avatar form, becoming a manifestation of Ares's wrath.
      • Mechanics:
        • Your melee weapon attacks deal an additional 4d12 slashing damage.
        • You become immune to being frightened or charmed.
        • You gain advantage on saving throws against spells and effects that target you.
        • Last until damage is taken

Artemis, Goddess of the Hunt:

    Earning Piety:

    • Healing a sick or injured wild animal
    • Stopping those who hunt for sport or profit
    • Proving your worth in a contest of archery
    • Slaying an aberration, a fiend, or an undead

    Losing Piety:

    • Killing an animal for any reason other than necessity
    • Dedicating a building or making a sacrifice to any God, including Artemis
    • Protecting a polis or farm from natural dangers

Piety Awards:

  • Artemis’s Acolyte (3+ Piety)
    • You can cast hunter’s mark, giving you the ability to deal an extra 1d6 damage on a successful hit on a target within 90ft. You can cast this spell a number of times equal to your Wisdom modifier. Replenishes on a successful long rest.
  • Artemis’s Disciple (10+ Piety)
    • You can cast speak with animals, giving you the ability to comprehend and communicate with any beast. You can cast this spell a number of times equal to your Wisdom modifier. Replenishes on a successful long rest. In addition, you have advantage on saving throws against being poisoned.
  • Pathfinder of Artemis (25+ Piety)
    • When a beast is about to attack you, it must make a DC 15 Wisdom saving throw. On fail, the creature must choose a different target, or the attack automatically misses you. On a successful save, the creature is immune to this ability for 24 hours.
    • Hunter’s Sanctuary (Action):
      • Effect: Gain the ability to create a sanctuary in the wilderness.
      • Mechanics:
        • You can create a safe haven in the form of a magical grove or hidden shelter where you and your allies can rest safely.
        • Sanctuary spans a 100ft radius
  • Champion of the Forest (50+ Piety)
    • You can increase your Dexterity or Wisdom score by 4 and also increase your maximum for that score by 4.
    • Artemis’s Wrath (Action):
      • Effect: Once per long rest, you can invoke Artemis's wrath to become an avatar of the hunt.
      • Mechanics:
        • Your ranged attacks automatically hit and deal maximum damage for a duration of one minute.
        • You gain advantage on all Survival and Perception checks during this time.

    Losing Piety:

    • Destroying a civic institution or sowing chaos within a city
    • Willfully breaking just laws for personal gain

Piety Awards:

  • Athena’s Scholar (3+ Piety)

    • You can cast speak with dead, giving you the ability to gain the intelligence of a corpse within 10ft, allowing it to answer any questions you pose. Replenishes on a successful long rest.
  • Athena’s Avatar (25+ Piety)
    • You can cast false life, giving you the ability to gain 25 temporary hit points. Replenishes on a successful long rest.
    • Athena’s Shied (Bonus Action):
      • Effect: Once per long rest, you can invoke Athena's protection to create a magical barrier.
      • Mechanics:
  • Champion of Knowledge (50+ Piety)
    • You can increase your Intelligence or Wisdom score by 4 and also increase your maximum for that score by 4.
    • Athena’s Inspiration (Action):
      • Effect: Once per long rest, you can inspire your allies with Athena's wisdom.
      • Mechanics:
        • Roll a d10 to determine the number of allies you can inspire.
        • The inspired allies gain temporary hit points equal to your Wisdom modifier times 10 and gain advantage on their next attack roll or ability check.
