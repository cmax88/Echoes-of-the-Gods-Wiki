Kiffisia



Description

Kiffisia is the Northern municipality of Athena, populated by the wealthy and the privileged. It is speckled with marble and gold-plated buildings that shine with a radiant light that can be seen from many cities across Gaia. Scholars and government members reside in these homes and can often be found indulging in the local activities, whether it be studying in the Academy of Plato, or exercising at the Olypieum. These citizens control the water distribution in the city, oftentimes unfairly giving more to one section of the city than the other, just to watch chaos ensue. Plutus, God of Wealth, had an immense hatred for Athena. When he saw how upset she was with Hephaestus after his accidental stunt, he was inspired to pull a stunt of his own. Upon Athena’s leave, Plutus snuck to her land and scattered a satchel of coins all across, covering the majority of the North with coinage. Once Athena returned, she became outraged at the sight and decided to return to Mount Olympus to the Hall of the Gods, vowing never to return to the mortal realm again. These coins were eventually reclaimed by Gaia in the years to come and erected into the buildings that now span across Kiffisia. Plutus is now worshipped in Kiffisia, at the Temple of Plutus, revered for the good fortune he gifted to the city of Athena.

Major Locations

Academy of Plato: This academy is the first and only school in Athena. Its beautiful structure captures the eyes of those who pass by. This institution offers a vast knowledge of what is known of the Gods and of the myths and lore of Gaia

Temple of Plutus: This temple sits by the Illithos River at the Western edge of the municipality. It is dedicated to the God Plutus, God of Wealth. It is the smallest temple in Athena but holds great value to the people of Kiffisia.

Lesser Locations



Aristaeus Plaza: The Aristaeus Plaza is a beautifully crafted public square located in Kiffisia, the affluent district of Athena. Surrounded by stunning buildings and fountains, the plaza is the epitome of luxury and grandeur. People from all over the city gather here to socialize, conduct business, and admire the stunning architecture. The statue of Aristaeus, God of the Shepherds, stands tall in the central fountain of the plaza, symbolizing the prosperity and success of the city's upper class.



Library of Pantainos: The Library of Pantainos, located in the heart of the wealthy district of Kifissia, is a magnificent building. Its grand facade is made of white marble columns, and its entrance is graced by a beautiful arch. Inside, the library is filled with shelves upon shelves of books, covering everything from ancient myths and legends to contemporary literature and knowledge. Visitors are welcomed with a quiet and serene atmosphere, making it the perfect place for scholars and book lovers to peruse and study in peace.



Thespian Terraces: The Thespian Terraces in Kiffisia are a series of luxurious balconies that offer breathtaking views of the city. With their elegant marble columns and intricate frescoes, they are a symbol of the wealth and power of the elite citizens of Athena. The terraces are often used for grand events and parties, where guests can enjoy the finest food, wine, and entertainment. Whether it's for a celebration, a business meeting, or a simple afternoon of relaxation, the Thespian Terraces are a must-see for anyone visiting the rich district of Athena.



Xenia Square: Xenia Square is located in the heart of the wealthy district of Kiffisia in Athena. It is a grand open space, surrounded by luxurious homes and stately buildings. The cobblestone pavement is lined with elegant trees, providing shade and beauty to the area. A grand fountain sits in the center of the square, depicting the God Zeus and his wife Hera. The square is a popular gathering spot for the wealthy citizens of Kiffisia, where they can socialize and conduct business.
