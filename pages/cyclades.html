The Cyclades

The Cyclades, a stunning archipelago in the heart of Gaia's Aegean Sea, is a region of breathtaking beauty and deep cultural heritage. Each island, with its unique landscapes and traditions, contributes to the rich mosaic of Cycladic life. Amidst these islands lie the cities of Lindos and Rhodes, each a beacon of civilization's enduring strength and creativity. Here, humans, Panes, and Panotiis coexist, their lives interwoven with the magic and mystery of the archipelago.

The Cities

  • Lindos: Situated on the island of Rhodes, Lindos is a city of sun-drenched shores and ancient ruins. Its acropolis stands as a testament to human ingenuity and the divine, overlooking a bustling market that attracts traders from across the seas.
  • Rhodes: The city of Rhodes is a marvel of architecture and engineering, famous for its Colossus that once guarded the harbor. Now, it's a melting pot of cultures and races, where magic and technology blend seamlessly.
