Agora



Description

Agora is the Westernmost municipality of Athena and the grand market of the city. If you’re not a scholar or a government official, this is where you’ll make a good living. There are many different cultures and peoples represented here, all of which display a unique trade and a great sensibility. No one here is afraid to get into a tussle if they find the need and are hardy hagglers who are not easily persuaded. When Hermes, the God of Travel and Commerce, was trying to win over the heart of Athena, he would visit her day in and day out, giving Athena gifts to try and persuade her love. Although Athena had no interest in Hermes as a partner, she played into his game and allowed his gifts to pile up in the West of her land. Once she had left to return to Mount Olympus, the gifts were left behind. Of the gifts were many types of ships, horses, wagons, woods, metals, and stones. The mortals used these gifts to trade with other blossoming civilizations and quickly became an early center of commerce in Gaia. Hermes is now worshipped in his Temple of Agora, for giving the Athenians the gift of travel and commerce.

Lesser Locations



Pygmalion’s Sculptor: The sculptor’s home is located in the bustling market district of Agora. It is a modest, two-story building with a wooden exterior and a clay tile roof. The front of the home features a large display window, showcasing some of Pygmalion's finest sculptures. The interior is filled with tools of the trade and an array of half-finished sculptures waiting to be brought to life.


The Chitirios: The Chitirios is a bustling marketplace in the Agora district of Athena. The market is filled with vendors selling goods from all over the land, from exotic spices and textiles to handmade pottery and jewelry. The sound of haggling and the scent of freshly baked goods fills the air as shoppers wander from stall to stall, searching for the best deals. The Chitirios is known as the heart of the market district, where locals and travelers alike come to buy, sell, and trade goods.



Senate House: The Senate House is a grand building located in the heart of Agora, the market district of Athena. It is a symbol of power and authority, with its imposing columns and white marble facade. The Senate House is where the elected leaders of Athena gather to discuss and make important decisions for the city. Inside, there are spacious chambers and ornate halls, with intricate frescoes and sculptures adorning the walls. The Senate House is a beacon of democracy, reflecting the proud history and rich culture of Athena.
