The Prophecy

Passage One:

“Listen well, brave heroes of old,
A journey lies ahead, stories untold.
A world in peril, you must defend,
Against the Gods, your journey must bend.

The first challenge you must overcome,
To the city of Thebes, you must become.
Where the daughters of Zeus wreck the lands,
And the fate of mortals’ rests in your hands.

The Muses of nine hold the key,
To power and glory, for you and me.
Find them all, and destroy with might,
And the path to victory will come to sight."

Passage Two:

“Oh, weary travelers, hear the words I say,
A task awaits you, on thine way.
A God of Illusion, Dolos, so bold,
His presence felt, in cities young and old.

His power's growing, causing fear and strife,
As mortals suffer, with each and every life.
The world you know is but his play,
And so, you must, this power take away.

Your journey leads, to the land of gold,
Where wealth and fame, are often told.
A city of merchants, where goods are sold,
But beware, for Dolos, has taken hold.

In the market square, where merchants haggle and trade,
His illusions have taken, all they have made.
You must discover, the source of his power,
And with it, his hold, you must devour.

Oh, brave adventurers, heed this call,
For victory, you shall stand tall.
With skill and might, you must succeed,
And the third passage, you shall indeed read.”

Third Passage:

“The third quest will lead to the fall,
Of light that shines, brighter than all.
With eyes of gold and a voice so sweet,
Apollo's power can't be beat.

The force that shields the God of Light,
Is guarded well, with all its might.
But a clue's been given, in the dark of night,
That holds the key, to the end of the fight.

A journey long, and a challenge great,
To defeat Apollo, will be your fate.
With courage strong and a heart so pure,
You must face the challenge that lies ahead, I'm sure.

In Delphi, the city of Apollo's name,
You'll find his temple and the answer to the game.
With this trial finished, and with Apollo’s defeat,
You'll be one step closer, to being complete.”

Fourth Passage:

“Listen well, brave adventurers, to what I say,
A quest to Korinthos, is where you must lay.
A city of art and architecture awaits,
But Athena, the wisdom goddess, dominates.

She’s destroying her own creations with glee,
And residing within the Temple of the Hill, she will be.
Defended by Otus and Tyto, the Owls so grand,
But defeat them and Erichthonius will lend a hand.

The son of Athena, imprisoned so unfair,
But from Otus’s belly, he will be aware.
He’ll share the goddess’s weaknesses and might,
Her disgust of men, her affection for light.

The Tome of Demise, she guards with all care,
A chronicle ancient, with knowledge beyond compare.
So, hurry and defeat her, with strategy so true,
And find the truth, to what you must do.

The protection of Gaia, may be in sight,
For the Gods are dethroned, with every fight.
So, seek and discover, the truth of the land,
And bring forth the victory, with thine sword in hand.”
